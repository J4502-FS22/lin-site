Phoebe Bridgers' 7 most emotionally devastating songs, ranked from least to most damaging

The indie singer-songwriter turned Tiktok’s sadgirl darling has a small but expensive discography that covers a range of sensitive subjects. If you’re looking to minimize time and maximize tears, check out these seven songs to get a good cry (or, at the very least, a minor existential crisis) in.

1. Steamroller

Aren’t we all tired of being sad? An early track of Bridgers’, she proves she doesn’t need anything more than 6 strings and a couple rhyming couplets about being incurably in love with a friend to hurt your feelings.

Best lyrics: “You’re a brother to me/You’re my partner in crime/You’re the feeling I get when I’m feeling fine.”

2. Moon Song

Moon Song might be the internet’s favorite sad Phoebe Bridgers song, and for good reason: the lyrics are simple but poignant, the melody is compelling and the subject of this song is rumored to be Bridgers’ bandmate and frontman of Bright Eyes, Conor Oberst.

Best lyrics: “You are sick/And you’re married/And you might be dying/But you’re holding me like water in your hands.”

3. Funeral

When Phoebe Bridgers performs this song live, she tells her audience starkly, “This song is about death.” And it is. And also, it isn’t. She sings eloquently about depression, dreams and her childhood bedroom with a muted earnestness that comes from her doubled vocal line.

Best lyrics: “Jesus Christ, I’m so blue all the time/And that’s just how I feel/Always have and I always will.”

4. Halloween

Often overlooked on Bridgers’ sophomore album, Punisher, Halloween exemplifies Bridgers’ ability to sing about something by not really singing about it. Over the course of the song, using references and allusions, Bridgers likens the story of a “ dead relationship” to Halloween over an uneven, off-putting instrumental line.

Best lyrics: “Always surprised by what I’d do for love/Some things I never expect/They killed a fan down by the stadium/Was only visiting, they beat him to death.”

5. Me & My Dog

Who knew a Black pug could be the source of so much strife? Me & My Dog, a song Bridgers wrote in collaboration with Lucy Dacus and Julien Baker (fellow members of the girl group, boygenius) absolutely violates. Clipped lyrics that sound like they’re coming out between sobs, ghostly backup vocals and a powerful, belted final line make this song downright difficult to listen to.

Best lyrics: “I wanna be emaciated/I want to hear one song without thinking of you/I wish I was on a spaceship/Just me and my dog and an impossible view.”

6. Scott Street

Scott Street has possibly the greatest opening two lines and outro of any song in her discography. Deceptively simple lyrics narrate walking down a street and catching up with an old friend. “I guess ‘Scott Street’ is about being really lonely,” Bridgers told Bandcamp in 2017. “Sometimes I want to cop out and say it’s about a lot of different things, but really it’s all there. It’s just a diary.”

Best lyrics: “Walking Scott Street, feeling like a stranger/With an open heart, open container.”

7. I Know the End

The earth-shaking finale to Punisher is more than just a song. It’s an experience. The song begins with Bridgers’ trademark muted vocals, speeds up and builds into a screaming wave of sound. It’s impossible to listen to without being affected by how seamlessly she navigates the end of the world.

Best lyrics: “AAAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHH.”

Ellie Lin 2022.
